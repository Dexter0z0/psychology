1. Motives vs. Needs: The Core Concepts

Need: The Deficiency

A state of lack or requirement for well-being.

The internal source of motivation.

Examples: Hunger, Thirst, Need for Connection.

Motive: The Driving Force

The conscious or unconscious reason for action.

The directed push that emerges from a need.

Examples: The drive to find food, the urge to call a friend.

2. Types of Motives: By Origin & Content

By Origin

Innate (Biological): Unlearned, physiological (e.g., hunger, thirst).

Learned (Social): Acquired through experience (e.g., achievement, power).

By Content (Kovalev)

Motives of Attraction: Driven towards a positive state (e.g., success, love).

Motives of Avoidance: Driven away from a negative state (e.g., failure, shame).

2. Types of Motives: By Awareness

By Level of Awareness

Conscious Motives

We are fully aware of what drives us.

Example: "I am working late to get a promotion."

Unconscious Motives

Hidden drivers that influence behavior outside our awareness.

Example: A deep-seated need for approval shaping career choices.

3. Hierarchy of Motives: Maslow's Pyramid

(From Base to Peak)

  1. Physiological Needs: Food, water, warmth, rest.
  2. Safety Needs: Security, safety, stability.
  3. Love & Belongingness: Relationships, friends, family.
  4. Esteem Needs: Respect, recognition, self-confidence.
  5. Self-Actualization: Achieving one's full potential.

4. The Emotional-Volitional Sphere

The Two Components:

The Emotional Component (The Fuel)

Subjective reactions (Joy, Fear, Anger).

Function: Signals significance, energizes behavior, communicates.

Human System

The Volitional Component (The Steering Wheel)

Will, self-control, deliberate action.

Function: Goal-setting, planning, overcoming obstacles.

Key Takeaways

  • Needs are deficiencies; Motives are the directed forces to satisfy them.
  • Motives can be Innate/Learned, for Attraction/Avoidance, and Conscious/Unconscious.
  • Motives exist in a Hierarchy (Maslow); basic needs come first.
  • Personality is driven by the Emotional-Volitional Sphere: Emotion is the fuel, Will is the steering wheel.
